# Cybersecurity Threat Actors

## Objective
Understand different types of cyber attackers, their motivations, and how internal and external threats can impact organizations.

## Types of Attackers

### Script Kiddies
Inexperienced attackers who use existing tools or scripts to launch attacks.

### White Hat Hackers
Authorized security professionals who identify vulnerabilities to improve security.

### Gray Hat Hackers
Attackers who identify vulnerabilities without authorization and may publicly disclose findings.

### Black Hat Hackers
Malicious attackers who exploit vulnerabilities for financial, personal, or political gain.

### Organized Attackers
Highly sophisticated groups including cybercriminal organizations, hacktivists, terrorists, and state-sponsored attackers.


## Internal Threats
Internal threats may originate from employees, contractors, or trusted partners.

### Possible Risks
- misuse of access privileges
- accidental malware infections
- unauthorized access
- data mishandling

## External Threats
External threats originate from attackers outside the organization.

### Possible Risks
- phishing attacks
- exploitation of vulnerabilities
- unauthorized system access
- social engineering attacks


## Security Observation
Improper removal of former employee access can create insider security risks and unauthorized access opportunities.


## SOC Relevance
SOC analysts monitor suspicious login activity, access misuse, phishing attempts, and unauthorized access events to help identify potential security incidents.

## Key Learning
Cyber attackers have different motivations and capabilities. Organizations must monitor both internal and external threats and apply strong access control and security monitoring practices.
